Doing this thing cause it seems fun

Feb. 11, 2025. link

1. Please introduce yourself.
I'm Valkin
2. How long have you been making websites?
I made my first website in... summer of 2023? I don't exactly remember when.
3. And what got you into the hobby?
My dad, he does programming for work, and it inspired me to grow. I learned some HTML, forgot it, learned some Python, forgot it, relearned both, actually remembered this time, then moved into Game dev, and this tiny corner of the web.
4. What kind of website are you most interested in?
I love the kind of website that is just that. A website. No complications, no ads, no accounts. Just use, or someone on the webs safe space.
5. What's your workflow? Do you plan your websites out thoroughly or do you come up with the design as you go along?
Inspiration is huge. I have most of the sites that inspred me on my button wall, and I sorta just... try to do what they do. With style and structure mainly.
6. Please link to your biggest inspirations.
Solaria. This website is what go me into this whole thing. Huge inspiration, I just love the way its styled and written.
7. What's your favourite part about making websites?
The sense that I've actually made something, something that truly, really, will have a space on the web, and the space is mine.
8. And the thing you struggle with the most?
Off. I'm fine on the content, but there is just so much structure and style. Source code on websites has been a huge help, but its hard. Flexbox, display type. Its just hard.
9. Do you keep the same layout on all of your pages? Or do you use different ones?
Mainly I keep the same layout, because its easy, and I find it makes page transistions less jarring, for only the content to change. I have considered using frames for the center content, that way I can have eveything be on the same page.
10. How confident are you with CSS?
Not very confident ar all. Styling is hard, but everytime I try, I learn something new. Progress!
11. Do you know how to correctly use <dl>?
Oh yeah. Definitly one of my favorite elements. It makes organizing everything easy and digestible.
12. What is your favourite HTML element?
Honestly, <dl>, but I do love details as well. Before I had this annoyingly complex dropdown system.
13. If you're making a new web page from scratch, what is the first thing you do?
Search the internet for the base html thing. I like maing pages, but always forget how to link styles, css, and where to put DOCTYPE
14. Do you know JavaScript?
Some. I am learning some as a game framework, and could probably get better with time, but its just not what I really do. ¯\_(ツ)_/¯
15. How about PHP?
Pff. Nothing at all. PHP is the kind of stuff I have never learned, nor do I ever want to interact with.
16. Does your website have a theme that you stick to?
Generally yes. I hate making different files for every page, so making files for themes themselves is just easier. I use this (honestly somewhat nasty) purple and green "hacker" style, but I have recently evolved, and love the warmer look in my most recent style.
17. Are you more focused on content or design?
Both are super important. However, content I will always somewhat prefer. I need some basic style, so barf doesn't happen, but after that, content all the way.
18. Do you own a domain name? If not, would you ever want to?
Yes. You are on it. Generally I own it because I like the fact that its mine, and can never be taken. It also just feels very official, which makes me feel very nice and happy.
19. What do you think of nostalgia-focused or "retro" websites?
Love them. The internet is to baby-proofed nowadays. Love corners.
20. Is your HTML valid? Do you even check?
Ummmmm. I plead the Fifth.
21. What are your opinion on buttons and banners?
They seem pretty cool. Buttons I have on my pages in my button wall are pretty cool websites, and I hope that I can help it grow. IDK what this means by banner, but ¯\_(ツ)_/¯.
22. What do you think of button walls in particular?
They seem pretty nice, like a screenshot of someone elses post. They are what connect us, and while they can sometimes feel cluttered, I love the organized chaos of them, not the seamless connections of the modern web.
23. If you started over again, would you make something similar or completely different?
Completely different. There are so many cool websites that approach pages not as pages, but as world in themselves, which makes them feel like a game. One day, I aspire to make something like that.
24. Are you envious of other people's websites?
Not really. I see them as inspiration, and if I was envious, I would just stew in envy, which would only make my corner of the web worse.
25. What text editor do you use?
VSCode, for built in git. Otherwise, I would just use a more simple one, with less AI.
26. Why do you use that one?
Git is hard. VSCode makes it easy.
27. Do you host your image files on your web server, or on another host?
On my web server. I can understand how some people host somewhere else, but I like my website self contained. The only exeption I have is my button wall, when peoples sites tell me that I "can just paste the code in." Love the convenience.
28. This might not be relevant to you, but what's your opinion on the Neocities vs. Nekoweb debate?
Whats Nekoweb?
29. How much server space would you estimate your main website takes up?
No more than 10 megabytes. I like things clean and messy, in a overall organized yet chaotic way. Like when your room is messy, but you know where everything is.
30. Do you keep local backups of your files?
No. Git is the way to go for me. It means on any computer, I can make changes. It has a kind of modularity to me.
31. Do you prefer simple or highly visual websites?
I love them both. Simple has its place, especially with the modern web having so much clutter with ads and buttons that barely work, but I will always take some eye candy.
32. Do you stick to certain colours? Do you do that on purpose, or is it your subconscious?
I ask my friends! My newest theme (as of the time of writing this), beige, is super nice, and I never would have thought of it if not for a friends advice.
33. Have you ever thought about quitting? Why?
Quitting what? This website? Coding? Programming? I have no job truly. I hope to get one in the future. But throughout it all, I will never, never quit creating with my keyboard, or my words, or my pencil. I don't need some dumb AI to do it for me.
34. Do you have many webmaster friends, or is it a solitary hobby?
Sadly no. The term webmaster is new to me, but I do not have many friend with websites. I am currently in process of convinving a friend that its worth it, and boy am I close. Ask me again in like, a week.
35. Do people in your real life know about your website?
Yes. Sadly. School is packed, so everyone in all my classes hear when I talk about it. Still, nothing I can do. ¯\_(ツ)_/¯
36. Do you update your website very often? How often is "very often"?
As of recently, yes. Multiple changes per day, sometimes, during the night as well. Before... not so much. Just for a school project here and there.
37. And the overall design, do you change that much? Why or why not?
Recently, I did a whole revamp, which I am loving so much. It is beautiful to see my website finally grow. I had a pretty cool glitch website before, but Glitch is dead. ¯\_(ツ)_/¯
38. Is your website more you-focused, hobby-focused, or outside world-focused?
Definently Me and Hobby focused. The outside world can stay outside. Here is my oasis of love in the desert of horrible news.
39. Do you do web design professionally?
No. Still in school.

It would be pretty nice. I hope to one day work in a job that requires coding, ideally game dev, but I would also be happy as a software engineer or web developer.

Its got great tab tiling, aboslutly amazing customization, and vertical tabs! It is just the best chromium. (In my opinion)
